Current version 1.4.0

v1.4.0

I've added a new options hub and improvements to the tool's structure.

  • New options hub: Thanks to your suggestion, we now have a chart options hub for report generation. You can choose between Top Tracks, Top Artists, and Top Albums to display on the shareable card.
    (Minimum: 1 chart — Maximum: 2 charts)
  • Tool restructuring: We redesigned part of the website’s internal structure due to the increase in features. This does not change anything for the end user, but it keeps things more organized behind the scenes.

Suggestion notes:

  • Hide profile: The goal of the tool is to create shareable cards for Last.fm users, and the profile is part of that identity. Adding a hide-profile option isn’t feasible right now due to layout rules and card structure. For now, the profile will remain, but you can crop the image after generating your card.
  • Add a minute counter: I'm working on a secondary tool where you’ll be able to generate reports based on total minutes/hours listened instead of scrobbles. More news soon.

You can also leave an idea or suggestion on our Feedback & Support page.

v1.3.0

New format option added

  • New format added: The 1x1 square format is now available for generating reports. It works with every time period option and is ideal for feeds or any platform that supports square posts.
  • UI and performance improvements: General interface polish and faster overall loading.

v1.2.0

Addition of new filter and addition of new colors

  • Added week filter: You can now filter your scrobbles from the last 7 days. Stories created with this filter are labeled as “My Week.”
  • New colors in the palette: I’ve added more options to the story color palette. You now have eight accent colors to choose from when generating your reports.

v1.1.0

Addition of new annual filter

  • Added annual filter: You can now see your stats for a full year. When you generate a story with the annual filter selected, you’ll get an annual card showing how many scrobbles and songs you listened to in the last 365 days (1 year). It’s perfect to use at the end of each year as your yearly recap.
